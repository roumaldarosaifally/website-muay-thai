03Equipment

Tools of the trade.

A true Nak Muay requires the proper armor. Training relies heavily on 16oz gloves for safe sparring, thick shin guards for checking kicks, and durable heavy bags for conditioning the eight limbs.

01

GLOVES

10-16oz leather boxing gloves. Heavier for sparring, lighter for competition — protect the hand you punch with.

02

HAND WRAPS

4-5m of cotton wrap that stabilises the wrist and knuckles under the glove. Non-negotiable before any bag work.

03

SHIN GUARDS

Foam-and-leather sleeves for sparring. Save your partner and save the shin you spent years conditioning.

04

MOUTHGUARD

Boil-and-bite or custom fit. Elbows land. Teeth do not grow back.

05

GROIN CUP

Steel or hard plastic — mandatory for men in every gym and every fight. Kicks stray.

06

MUAY THAI SHORTS

Wide-cut satin shorts allow full hip rotation for kicks and knees. The colour and script often mark the gym.

07

MONGKOL & PRAJIOUD

Sacred headband and armband blessed by a monk or trainer. Worn during the Wai Kru; considered spiritually protective.

08

HEAVY BAG

The 40-70kg banana bag is the daily grindstone — kicks, knees, and teeps by the hundred.

09

FOCUS PADS & THAI PADS

The trainer's tools. Thai pads absorb kicks and knees; focus mitts sharpen hand speed and reactions.
